Here’s a **clean bullet-list** of the most common **business & technical request types**, grouped by usage area.

---

## **General Business / Procurement**

* **RFI – Request for Information**
  Used to gather information about vendors, capabilities, or solutions

* **RFP – Request for Proposal**
  Vendors submit formal proposals describing *how* they would solve a problem (scope, timeline, cost)

* **RFQ – Request for Quote** *(or Request for Quotation)*
  Focused on pricing — used when requirements are known and comparable

* **RFT – Request for Tender**
  Formal solicitation inviting qualified suppliers to bid on work (often public sector)

* **ITT – Invitation to Tender**
  Variation of RFT, formal invite to selected vendors

* **EOI – Expression of Interest**
  Used to find vendors willing/interested before issuing RFP/RFT

* **BAFO – Best And Final Offer**
  Follow-up request after proposals are reviewed, asking vendors to sharpen pricing and scope

* **LOI – Letter of Intent**
  Not binding, signals intent to proceed before contract negotiation

---

## **Internal Business / Strategy Inputs**

* **BRD – Business Requirements Document request**
  Request or trigger to document requirements before execution

* **BCR – Business Case Request**
  Stakeholders request justification for funding or proceeding

* **SOW – Statement of Work Request**
  Internal ask to define scope, deliverables, and boundaries of work

---

## **Product & Engineering Governance**

* **RFC – Request for Comments**
  Used to collect feedback on design proposals, architecture, or changes

* **ADR – Architecture Decision Record (not a request itself, but often triggered by RFC)**
  Captures a final decision after discussions

* **FR – Feature Request**
  Submitted by users, product managers, or developers for new functionality

* **CR / CRQ – Change Request**
  Formal request to modify scope, schedule, requirements, or configuration

* **ECR – Engineering Change Request**
  Common in manufacturing/IT hardware to control configuration changes

* **TCR – Technical Change Request**
  Narrower engineering changes, config updates, system modifications

* **PIR – Post-Implementation Review Request**
  Triggered after deployments or project completion to analyze outcomes

---

## **IT, Security & Compliance**

* **IR – Incident Report / Incident Request**
  Documents unplanned disruptions and requests resolution

* **SR – Service Request**
  Day-to-day IT service asks (access, installs, account changes)

* **RBAC / Access Request (AR)**
  Formal request for role-based permissions or data/system access

* **RTP – Request to Procure (IT purchasing)**
  Internal trigger to buy equipment, licenses, or services

* **DPIA Request – Data Protection Impact Assessment**
  Required before handling regulated personal data (GDPR/PII contexts)

---

## **Finance & Procurement Control**

* **PO Request – Purchase Order Request**
  Enables purchasing and budget authorization

* **CAPEX Request – Capital Expenditure Approval**
  For long-term investment purchases

* **OPEX Request – Operating Expense Approval**
  For recurring expenses and operational spending

---

## **Human Resources & Operations**

* **Hiring Requisition (Req)**
  Formal request to open a hiring position

* **Training Request**
  Ask for professional development or certification funding

* **Travel Request**
  Approval required for business trips or off-site events
