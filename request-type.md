# **One-Page Cheat Sheet**

*(All the acronyms + when to use them — one screen, no scrolling)*

---

### **PROCUREMENT REQUESTS**

* **RFI** – Gather general vendor info
* **EOI** – Find out who’s interested
* **RFP** – Ask for full solution proposals
* **RFQ** – Request detailed pricing
* **RFT / ITT** – Formal bid submission (common in government)
* **BAFO** – Ask finalists to sharpen cost/scope
* **LOI** – Declare intent to proceed (not binding)
* **PO Request** – Approve a purchase and trigger ordering

---

### **PROJECT & PORTFOLIO**

* **BRD** – Document business requirements
* **BCR** – Write the business justification
* **SOW Request** – Define scope, deliverables, constraints
* **CAPEX / OPEX Requests** – Decide on budget category

---

### **PRODUCT & ENGINEERING**

* **RFC** – Socialize a proposal, collect feedback
* **FR (Feature Request)** – Propose enhancement/new capability
* **CR / TCR / ECR** – Request change to scope/config/code
* **ADR** – Capture the final decision from RFC discussion
* **PIR Request** – Request a post-project/post-release review

---

### **IT / SECURITY / COMPLIANCE**

* **SR (Service Request)** – Routine IT ask (access, install, reset)
* **IR (Incident Request)** – Report outage/security issue
* **Access Request** – Ask for system/data permissions
* **DPIA Request** – Evaluate privacy risk before processing PII
* **RTP (Request to Procure)** – Kick off IT purchasing

---

### **HR & OPERATIONS**

* **Hiring Requisition** – Open a new job role
* **Training Request** – Ask for certification or course funding
* **Travel Request** – Get approval for business travel

---

### **Lifecycle Usually Looks Like**

**RFI → EOI → RFP/RFQ/RFT → BAFO → SOW → PO → Delivery → PIR**
